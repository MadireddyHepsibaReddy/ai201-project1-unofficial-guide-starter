Review 1:
I loved this class! This was the most interactive course I've taken at UWM. Surprisingly, this course was more interactive than some of the more liberal arts courses I've taken! He's phenomenal! Class is NEVER boring, which is good because class participation is a percentage of your entire grade.

Review 2:
Good teacher. Focuses on student participation and exams. Make sure to understand the concepts well on a basic level, and pay attention to what he says in lectures as pretty much anything can show up on the exam. Participation matters. Test heavy. Graded by few things.

Review 3:
Overall, Abbas was a good professor. The class itself was more the concept of physics than actual physics. It can be fairly difficult at times, but in the long run it wasn't the worst. If you are going into a science-related field, I would recommend.

Review 4:
Abbas is my favorite professor thus far. Attendance is 25% of your grade. Homework is also 25%, but he doesn't grade overly hard. The other half is the final exam, which isn't the worst if you go to class and pay attention. He makes paying attention much easier than other teachers. Very nice and helpful if you need it.

Summary: Attendance is 25% of your grade — do not skip class. Homework is 25%, final exam is 50%. Lectures are highly interactive and concept-focused rather than formula-heavy. Most students enjoy the class. Good choice if you want an engaging physics experience, but be aware that class participation directly affects your grade.
