Review 1:
One of the worst professors I have had at UWM. She has very unclear learning objectives, and when asked clarifying questions, gets frustrated with students. Her slides are unhelpful, often containing no words and just uncaptioned pictures of proteins, so I had to rely solely on the book to learn anything.

Review 2:
Graded only on 4 exams with a 40% final. Exams are only 20 questions to assess for 6-7 chapters of material. She talks too fast and is very difficult to understand as her thoughts trail off and she mumbles the last of her sentence. States only broad ideas will be tested but asks very specific questions on exams.

Review 3:
Read the chapters, lecture notes, and meet with the professor if you want more than a passing grade in this course. As a heads up, her final exam is more difficult and nothing like her online exams.

Review 4:
Online course. The quizzes are okay, but she asks very specific questions and even when I take really detailed notes I don't know the answer.

Review 5:
To pass, you MUST look up test bank questions off Quizlet (she gets her test questions from there). Here are some Quizlets that helped me: kphelle, braun1725, Rawr_Ashaway, Ashybean99.

Review 6:
To put it simply, this class is a joke. The lecture slides are vague, lectures are not helpful, and there is no clear path on what you need to study to be successful. This is a class that burns out even the brightest students and you can expect to see lots of withdrawals from the course in the first couple weeks. UWM is letting the students down.

Summary: Consistently negative reviews. Graded on very few assessments (4 exams + 40% final). Slides lack detail. Exam questions are highly specific despite broad stated learning objectives. Students widely recommend using Quizlet test banks to survive the course.
